Photography & Games

However, I hold a more positive belief about games.

Zina Lu|May 23, 2021

It is not obvious at the first thought, about the possible connection between photography and game. But for me they are inherently connected, since they both serve as a method of visually constructing cognitive expressions and both are important ways of human communications.

Last year I went out and shot something quite incomplete by the sea course near my apartment, and later I posted the video and photos on my Tumblr blog.


Photography

My Tumblr Blog, Photography by Sea, Oct 2020

The appearance of a graphical interface is common and often essential for the delivery of a work of photography or digital interaction (my personal perception of ‘What is a Game’). Because we audiences have to attain some inputs in order to begin our own appreciation process, which is closely related and could be defined by the form and features of certain chosen median. In this case, a graphical or even a multidimensional interface.

When I was shooting outside, I am thinking about how I could express my messages with the help of digital images. To let audiences who didn’t physically experience what I have experienced can understand my messages as well, through their interaction of the digital interface.

This process frequently relates me to the creation of digital games, about the similarities and differences between the two genres. Obviously, games need more than merely visual information but also delicately designed mechanics of interaction. But those static images seem self-complete as well.


New interaction

It is interesting to think of the evolution of art formats. It seems legal to say game is just a new derivation of non-interactive art, from its old predecessors, such as movies, photography, and static novels. But do we really discover something new? Or maybe we are just rewriting what could be done succinctly with old genres in the name of ‘new interaction’.

However, I hold a more positive belief about games. When I gaze at those static images or flickering frames, as the artistic expression are they self-complete, but as audiences are we feeling being rejected from further conceptualization. Because what is behind the interface is always far beyond what is presented on the interface.


The final video. The messages are still unclear and confusing though I tried my best in editing, voicing and photographing.
